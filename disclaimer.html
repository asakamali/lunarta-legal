Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: July 2026

In an emergency, don't use this app. Call 911 (or your local emergency number), or the Poison Control hotline at 1-800-222-1222 (U.S.), immediately.

Not medical advice

Safebump summarizes publicly available guidance from sources like the FDA, USDA, CPSC, ACOG, EWG, and NIH, and uses AI to generate plain-language summaries. This content is for general informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

No doctor-patient relationship

Using Safebump does not create a doctor-patient relationship between you and Safebump, its developer, or any contributor. Always seek the advice of your physician, midwife, pharmacist, or other qualified health provider with any questions you have about a medical condition, medication, or product — especially before starting, stopping, or changing anything related to your health or your pregnancy.

AI-generated content can be wrong

Summaries in Safebump are generated using an AI language model based on general public health guidance. AI-generated content can be incomplete, outdated, or simply incorrect. Recall information is pulled from government databases that can also lag behind real-world events. Always verify anything important directly with your healthcare provider or the original source (e.g., fda.gov, cpsc.gov, fsis.usda.gov).

Individual circumstances vary

Safety recommendations can depend on your individual health history, medications, allergies, and stage of pregnancy or breastfeeding. General guidance shown in the app may not apply to your specific situation.

Your use, your judgment

By using Safebump, you acknowledge that you are using the information at your own discretion and risk, and that neither Safebump nor its developer is responsible for decisions made based on the app's content.
